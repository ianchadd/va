Before you begin solving puzzles, we'll walk through several examples to make sure that you understand how the puzzle works.

Example 1:

First, consider the following puzzle:

You'll rearrange the tiles by clicking on them, which will move the tile into the empty space. You can only move the tiles that are next to an empty cell and the tile you choose is automatically moved to the empty cell. For example, in this puzzle there are 3 moves you can do: move the tile with the 3 down, move the one with the 5 to the right, or move the tile with the 6 up.

Between the 3 moves, moving the tile with the number 6 upwards is the only correct move: by moving the 6 up, you can solve the puzzle. The other available moves do not help solve the puzzle.

When you click on a tile next to an empty cell, the tile will automatically be moved to the empty cell. So, in this case, you should click on the 6 to move it up.
