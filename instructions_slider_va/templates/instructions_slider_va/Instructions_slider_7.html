Since there is a time limit, it is best to solve the puzzle with the minimum number of moves.

For the remainder of this study, we'll say that a move is "correct" if it moves the puzzle closer to the solution.

We'll say that a move is "incorrect" if it moves the puzzle farther away from the solution.

There are no neutral moves: all moves are correct or incorrect.

To summarize: when you solve a puzzle, first arrange the cells of in the top row in the correct order, then arrange the cells in the left column in the correct order. Always try to solve the puzzle in as few moves as possible.
