Let's go back to the initial puzzle.

This time, let's try to move the 7 down.

Then move the 8 to the right, the 4 to the top and, finally, the 7 to the left. You have now arranged the left column in the correct order with only four moves.

We keep the left column as it is (as well as the top row).

Since there is a time limit, it is best to solve the puzzle with the minimum number of moves. We will define a move as "right" if it makes a puzzle more close to the solution, and a move as "wrong" if it makes a puzzle farthest from the solution. There are no neutral moves: all moves are right or wrong .


In summary: when you solve the puzzle, first, arranges the cells of the first row at the top in order correct, then proceed by arranging the cells of the column of left in the correct order. Always try to reduce the as many moves as possible.
